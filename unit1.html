1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT has a very significant role in modern business. As technology advances, it is being utilised more and more in business settings. For example, businesses use IT to their advantage for:

  • Market research;
    • Online surveys and social media presence are excellent methods of learning more about the company's customers.
  • Customer support;
    • Support for customers has become increasingly more accessible with the introduction of email, online chat support and support social media accounts.
  • Marketing;
    • Marketing has improved greatly through the use of IT. Businesses are able to reach a wider audience with things such as online advertising.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

There are several risks that might impact digital projects. One risk factor that impacts digital projects is failing to plan. Being unprepared for the unexpected and not addressing known risks can hinder the progress of a digital project. This is why it is important to plan ahead and have measures in place in case something does go wrong [1]. Another risk factor is lack of communication within a team. Communication allows the project to be completed quicker, more accurately and more efficiently as everyone understands their role.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

"The mutually agreed General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) came into force on May 25, 2018, and was designed to modernise laws that protect the personal information of individuals"[1]. These new laws can greatly affect digital projects. For example, digital businesses now have to design data flows and reduce collected data to the absolute minimum. Previously companies would have stored customer data for future use (marketing, sales, etc.), however that is no longer allowed. Any GDPR infringements may result in large financial sanctions, so digital businesses must be very mindful of how they handle customer’s data.[2]

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency

Productivity and efficiency can be improved by completely planning out exactly how you want the website to look, for example by drawing it out. By doing this you can be more organised and thus be more productive. Having a plan means you spend less time trying different things out because you already have an idea to execute. Another way to improve productivity and efficiency is to commit to one task at a time. Doing several tasks at once can slow you down and reduce accuracy.
